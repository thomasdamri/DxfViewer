DEF-00421/A Essential LIVE
Valve handle exhibits progressive misalignment during actuation
CV-100-XF · Crossfeed valve
mechanical-wear alignment
Defect description
Valve handle exhibits progressive misalignment during actuation, resulting in increased torque requirements and inconsistent positioning at end states. Observed deviation from nominal alignment exceeds acceptable tolerance thresholds under sustained cyclic load. Issue was first noted during routine inspection in Q4 2024 and has progressively worsened with each subsequent actuation cycle. Alignment offset at full open position now measures approximately 4.2° from centreline, compared to a design tolerance of ±1.5°.
View more
Defect nature
Mechanical wear and fatigue within the valve stem coupling assembly, likely from prolonged cyclic stress and insufficient lubrication. Secondary contributing factors may include minor deformation of the coupling socket under thermal expansion.
Solution
Isolate and depressurise the affected system prior to intervention. Disassemble the valve handle and inspect the stem coupling for signs of wear, deformation, or material degradation. Replace worn components per maintenance schedule.
